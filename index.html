About

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in the Electrical and Computer Engineering department at the University of California, San Diego. I am currently working with Prof. Yatish Turakhia on developing scalable phylogenetic-based algorithms for pathogen detection from noisy wastewater data, and machine learning models for evolution prediction. My research interests lie at the intersection of computer architecture, machine learning, and bioinformatics.

Prior to joining UC San Diego, I worked as a Digital Design Engineer at Texas Instruments, where I gained a deep understanding of the intricacies of silicon design. Additionally, I had the opportunity to spend two summers at Intel, working on architecture modeling for next-generation GPUs.
